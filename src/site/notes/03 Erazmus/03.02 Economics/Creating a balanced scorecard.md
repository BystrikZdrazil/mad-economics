# Step 1: Assessment
- Understand the organization’s current situation before designing the scorecard.
- Review existing plans, performance data, stakeholder expectations, and environmental factors.
- Identify strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats, and readiness for a strategic management system.
- Outcome: a clear diagnostic foundation for the Balanced Scorecard.

# Step 2: Strategy
- Clarify mission, vision, values, and strategic themes.
- Ensure the organization has a coherent, well‑defined strategy that can be translated into objectives.
- Outcome: a strategic foundation that guides all later steps.

# Step 3: Strategic Objectives
- Convert strategy into specific, action‑oriented objectives.
- Organize objectives into the four Balanced Scorecard perspectives:
    - Financial
    - Customer
    - Internal Process
    - Learning & Growth (Organizational Capacity)
- Outcome: a set of objectives describing what must be achieved to execute the strategy.

# Step 4: Strategy Mapping
- Create a visual map showing cause‑and‑effect relationships among objectives.
- Demonstrate how improvements in Learning & Growth enable better processes, which improve customer outcomes, which drive financial results.
- Outcome: a strategy map that communicates the logic of the strategy.

# Step 5: Performance Measures (KPIs)
- Select indicators that show whether each objective is being achieved.
- Use a balanced mix of leading and lagging measures.
- Ensure measures are meaningful, reliable, and aligned with strategy.
- Outcome: a set of KPIs that make the strategy measurable.

# Step 6: Strategic Initiatives
- Identify the projects and actions required to achieve the objectives.
- Prioritize initiatives and allocate resources accordingly.
- Outcome: a portfolio of initiatives that drive strategic change.

## Scorecard Rollout
- Communicate the enterprise‑level scorecard across the organization.
- Use the scorecard to build understanding, alignment, and engagement.
- Outcome: employees understand the strategy and how their work contributes.

# Step 7: Performance Analysis
- Establish a disciplined review process for analyzing KPI results.
- Monitor trends, diagnose problems, and adjust actions as needed.
- Outcome: a performance management rhythm that keeps strategy active.

# Step 8: Alignment (Cascading)
- Cascade the scorecard to business units, departments, teams, and sometimes individuals.
- Ensure lower‑level scorecards support the enterprise‑level strategy.
- Outcome: organization‑wide alignment and accountability.

# Step 9: Evaluation and Sustainment
- Integrate the Balanced Scorecard into budgeting, planning, and governance.
- Review and refine the system regularly to keep it relevant.
- Outcome: a sustained strategic management system that supports long‑term success.
