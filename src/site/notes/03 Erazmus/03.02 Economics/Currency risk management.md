# Exchange rate basics
- Floating exchange rates
	- Supply and demand for each currency
		- Companies trading internationally
		- Investors trading securities
		- Central bank interventions

- The **weaker the foreign currency** gets, the **stronger buying power** I hold and vice versa

**Bullish** means the expectation of rising FX

**Bearish** means the expectation of falling FX

# Import and export

> [!example]
> A weak currency provides a competitive advantages on the world market, and president Donald Trump was in no way satisfied that the dollar was the strangest currency on the market.
>
> In many ways, having a weak currency can be an advantage in the fight for economic growth. The country with the weakest currency had a competitive edge in the global market. Companies can sell their goods more easily, which in turn increases production and employment.


# Tools for managing FX risk
- Adjust price
- Require payment in home currency
	- Difficult with some customers
- Currency accounts
- Produce in US
- Hedge via forwards/options
- Sales timing is crucial to maximise profit and minimise unnecessary costs
- In some cases, eliminating currency risks may mean to move production to the buyer country

# Hedging with forward contracts
- The most commonly used method
- By entering the contract, the company locks in an exchange rate in advance
- Reduces or **eliminates the risk of exchange-rate fluctuations**

>[!info]+
>A currency forward contract is characterised by:
>- An exchange rate
>- An agreed amount of currency to be exchanged
>- A predetermined delivery date on which the transaction will take place

# Hedging with options
- Options can be bought and sold
- Risk limited to premium for buyers

|        | **Buy**       | **Sell**           |
| ------ | ------------- | ------------------ |
| *Call* | Right to buy  | Obligation to sell |
| *Put*  | Right to sell | Obligation to buy  |

> [!example]
> A company needs euros in one year. The forward exchange rate at that time is 1.2 dollars per euro. Instead, they can choose to buy a call option, which gives them the right to purchase euros at a maximum price.

## Options vs forwards
A company may choose to use options instead of forwards if:
- The company wants to benefit when exchange rates move in a fabourable direction
- The company wants to avoid being locked into a contract with a price above the market level
- The company is uncertain whether the transaction or need for foreign currency will actually occur
