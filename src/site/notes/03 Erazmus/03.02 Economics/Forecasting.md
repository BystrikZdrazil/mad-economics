# 3 overall types
1. **Qualitative** (*judgement/counting*) techniques
	- No or limited amount of data
	- Based on human judgement
2. **Time series analysis**
	- Many **years of data** available to identify:
		- Seasonal fluctuations
		- Cyclical fluctuations (2-3 years)
		- Trends
		- Growth/decline rates
3. **Causal models**
	- Includes historical data and finds relationships between internal data and external influences 
		- Political
		- Economic
		- Social and technological developments
		- Related/competing companies

# Possible forecasting methods and models

## Judgmental methods
- **Individual methods**
	- Decision rules
- **Group methods**
	- Role playing
- **Aggregate methods**
	- Premarket tests

## Technological methods
- **Extrapolative methods**
	- Growth curves
- **Expert based methods**
	- Delphi

## Quantitative methods
- **Time series**
	- Smoothing
- **Explanatory methods**
	- Regression
- **Monitoring approaches**

# Bias in forecasting
Bias/misunderstandings:
- **Wishful thinking**, looking for signs supporting what you already believe will happen
- **Pressure to deliver** convincing **forecasts** rather than realistic scenarios

![ECO_forecasting_7.png](/img/user/06%20Images/06.01%20Erazmus/ECO_forecasting_7.png)

# Improving forecasting
- Combine different forecast types
- Balance strengths and limitations
- Simulate different results
- Sensitivity analysis
- Use 'common sense'
- But beware of bias
