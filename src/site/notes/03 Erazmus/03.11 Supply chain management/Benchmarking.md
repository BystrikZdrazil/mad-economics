- **Evaluating** and **assessing** how and organisation’s development has progressed
	- It is necessary to be able to **measure the development**
	- Starting point must be known

# Modes of benchmarking
- **Quantitative**
	- Financial
	- Comparison results (*ROI, market share*), KPIs
	- Against competitors or other divisions or departments
- **Qualitative**
	- *“Best practices”*
	- Benchmarking against similar strategic alignment and or processes (*often in different industries*)

# Supply chain objectives
- **Waste reduction**
	- To be as clean and carbon-neutral as possible
- **Minimising time consumption**
	- Efficient movement of products
- **Increasing flexibility**
- **Reducing costs**

## Quantitative objectives
- To fully integrate SCM in the [[03 Erazmus/03.02 Economics/Balanced scorecard\|BSC]] framework, it’s important to incorporate quantitative measures

> [!example]
>- Supply chain efficiency
>	- Total value added time = **10**
>	- Total time in the supply chain = **50**
>	- Supply chain cycle efficiency = **10/50=0,2**
- **Number of choices offered** relative to response **time**
	- A measure showing the efficiency of the supply chains ability to offer variety without lengthening the time it takes to create this variety

>[!example]+
>- Measurement example
>	- Choices offered = **20**
>	- Response time = **4 days**
>	- Number of choices relative to response time = **20/4=5**
	
# The BS vs SCM
- **Customer Perspective**
	- **Goals**: customer view of product or service
	- **Measures**: number of customer contact points
- **Internal business perspective**
	- **Goals**: flexible response
	- **Measures**: Number of choices or average response time
