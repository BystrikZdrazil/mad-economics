# Risks and solutions
*Identify and prioritise at least 3 different risks/vulnerabilities that are important for Cocio to handle*
- **Risk of alternatives**
	- Larger companies may utilise economies of scale to beat Cocio
- **Changing consumer preferencs**
	- There is a fair amount of sugar in Cocio’s products, and people around the world are leaning more towards lower sugar consumption
- **Dependence on danish market**
	- While they are exporting, the backbone of their revenues is still the danish market
	- Denmark being a health-focused country may promote healthier alternatives to Cocio
- **Raw materials price volatility**
	- The cost of milk and cocoa can fluctuate, which may negatively impact Cocio
		- Especially with cocoa, as it is imported, so international relations play a big role in the production

*How should Cocio work with these vulnerabilities?*
- **Risk of alternatives**
	- Cocio may set up a trendy brand image, attractive to teenagers and young adults by promoting their products as something more as just a cocoa drink
		- For example, bringing in the social aspect of drinking Cocio with your friends after studying or similar
- **Changing consumer preferences**
	- Launching a lighter version of their product (*Cocio deLight*) or a Cocio protein drink targeted towards fitness people.
	- Cocio may also preach the health benefits of pure cocoa, as they are organic, explaining it may be helpful in regulated amounts
- **Dependence on danish market**
	- Try to internationalise to countries with the lowest barriers of entry and low risk
- **Raw materials price volatility**
	- Making contracts with suppliers, [[03 Erazmus/03.02 Economics/Commodities risk management#Hedging with contracts\|hedging with contracts]] or maybe even [[03 Erazmus/03.02 Economics/Commodities risk management#Hedging via vertical integration\|hedging via vertical integration]] if the budget allows it
