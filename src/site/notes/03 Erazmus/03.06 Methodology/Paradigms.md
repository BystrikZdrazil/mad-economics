- The alternatives to pragmatism are [[03 Erazmus/03.06 Methodology/Research onion#Positivism\|positivism]] and [[03 Erazmus/03.06 Methodology/Research onion#Interpretivism\|interpretivism]]

# Interpretivism
- Key to understanding when to use it, is the focus of **subjectivism**
- Humans are different
- Meaning is the key (not the words themselves)
- Everything must be considered/translated into context

NB  you can still use quantitative information, but like everything else in interpretivistic structures, it must be ‘translated’ into context (e.g., when analysing worker satisfaction, you might use unemployment rates (objective, rational) but you ‘translate’ the numbers as part of your analysis

## When is interpretivism most relevant?
1. Purely explorative phases
2. Social contexts where no physical phenomenon exists
3. Research focusing on symbols (e.g., culture), cooperation, social interaction etc.

>[!info]+
> Be careful when you are doing early stages of explorative research
>
> You don’t know what you are looking for, so using e.g., surveys will be highly biased, since the questions would be based on YOUR PERSONAL ASSUMPTIONS….. (highly biased)
>
> The optimal way would be to split the research:
> 1. Explorative, inductive phase identifying variables
> 2. Evaluative, deductive phase testing variables in large population

# Why consider pragmatism
It is kind of a short-cut

When you can’t be bothered looking at how to match axiology, epistemology and ontology in the two different research structures, but basically just want to get on with solving the problem.

It is ‘the easy way’, not ‘the best way’…. And you have to argue for ‘easy’ being better than ‘best’.

You lose some control of your solution – harder to justify.

# Thematic VS narrative analysis
**Thematic analysis**: Based on the problem researched, all info is split according to themes.

>[!example]+
> - Personal characteristics
> - Duties
> - Activities

**Narrative analysis**: the guides are analysed focusing on what the author **actually** meant.
