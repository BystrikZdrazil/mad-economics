- **Research** -> **data** -> **analysis**
    - Within a research, we collect data/information, which then creates a base for the research
    - Having collected data, we can commence an analysis, which is based on the data we collected (hence the research)

# Basic division
- **Qualitative** - based on “*qualities*” = interviews, gathering information based on experiences/knowledge of others, that cannot be projected in numbers
- **Quantitative** - based on “*quantities*” = surveys, gathering information, which is based on numbers or a set range (Likert scale)

## Quantitative research
- Surveys
- Observation
- Calls

> [!imporatnt]
> Measured **in numbers**

### Methods used
- Likert scale
	- 1-10 rating
	- Strongly disagree - strongly agree
	- Yes / No / (*N/A*)

## Qualitative research
- **Focus group**
	- Few people are interviewed for a deep understanding
- **Observation**
	- Of a smaller focus group behaving in a certain situation

> [!imporatnt]
> **Cannot** be measured **in numbers**

### Ontology
- Being critical to reality
- *What is reality?*

### Epistemology
- Accepting **existing** theories

### Axiology
- Our own subjective thoughts / appreciation of society
- People are **affected by their upbringing**
- More subjective part of philosophy
- Carries the message of a certain population
	- Stereotypes for a certain country, their beliefs etc.

# Conceptual framework
- Works a bit like SWOT model
- Identifies the key factors (positive, negative) surrounding the problem area
- Advantage - it structures the work, gradually builds the report up while securing the considerations of a well-defined research approach
- The purpose:
    - How to identify key problems in a case/situation/company
    - How to prioritize and organize information/develop knowledge
    - Create an understanding of how to initiate research when entering into an internship/project-based assignments

## Literature review
- Look at the conclusion/findings of the literature review - what do we know?
    - Which factors are important to understand the stress students experience?
    - Which of our hypotheses were proved/discarded?
        - List the important factors (the ones to include, e.g. my factor of attitude to intelligence - born with or developed)
- How do the factors relate?
    - Are they connected, substituting… → short-list of factors to be included (clean them up so no factor is doubled)

## The agenda/process
- Prepare the overall research design - especially the approach (induction, deduction…) and purpose (explorative, descriptive, explorative…)
    - Key words and minimal description
    - Prepare the literature review -
        - What do I know about stress among students to start the research process (brainstorming)
        - Do I have any hypothesis (what do I think) - how do I prove these?
        - Are the factors related - can I split them in clusters? (e.g. factors dealing with job market, factors dealing with personality…)
- Find 2-3 good sources which have answers to the question and use this sort of a table:
    - One table a key word
