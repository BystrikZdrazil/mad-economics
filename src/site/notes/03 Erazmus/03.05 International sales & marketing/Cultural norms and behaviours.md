**Business culture** - externally based, customers, trading partners
- It is about dealing with people outside the company

**Company culture** - internally based, HRM
- It is about dealing with people inside the company

# Business culture and values
- Business culture is defined by **shared values** (*core benefits*) and **norms** (*unwritten rules*)
- These elements dictate **interaction styles**, **decision making** and **work ethic**, determining **employee engagement** and **organizational performance**

## 8 elements of business culture
1. Credibility
2. Respect
3. Fairness
4. Pride
5. Belonging
6. Effective leadership
7. Values
8. Innovation

# Company culture and values
- Company culture refers to the way an organization conducts business. It describes the formal and informal systems, behaviours and shared ethos that make up the work environment
- Company culture determines how employees and management interact and handle operations
- Company values are the principles and standards that guide business actions, decisions and behaviours
	- These principles inform employees, management and customers about their priorities, beliefs and commitments

## Examples of company cultures
**Hierarchy culture**
- Traditional culture
- Structure and control
- Clearly defined policies and procedures
- Implementing strict rules
- Monitoring employees

**Clan culture**
- Family culture
- Creating a friendly and supportive working environment
- Similar interests
- Commitment to wellbeing

**Adhocracy culture**
- Flexible and adaptable
- Constantly thinking ahead
- Fast-paced
- Energetic and creative work environment
- Experimenting with new ideas
- Might result in chaos

**Market culture**
- Focusing on results
- Work hard to achieve objectives
- Demanding but rewarding work environment
- Meeting objectives is main priority
- Increase financial growth

# Values VS culture
- **Implementation**
	- Company culture takes longer to implement, and change compared to company values
- **Impact**
	- Values and culture have varying impacts on an organization and its employees
	- Company values have a high impact on company culture
- **Benefits**
	- Company culture encourage engagement and participation
	- Business values aid decision-making processes
- **Consistency**
	- Company values are eternal and usually never change, culture adapts and changes with time

# Cultural symbols
- Cultural symbols are essential elements within societies that represent shared meanings, values and beliefs among members of a group
- The symbols can take different forms, including flags, words and monuments
- In a business context symbols are represented by brand names and logos
- They help individuals communicate and reinforce their identity within a culture

# Building a strong brand
- Choosing brand elements
	- **Brand name**
	- **Logo**
	- **Symbols**
		- Memorable
		- Meaningful
		- Likeable
		- Transferable
		- Adaptable
		- Protectable
- Communicating the brand and its symbolic value

## Cultural relativism
- Meanings derived from symbols can differ significantly across cultures
- An object like a flag may evoke patriotism in one context but represent oppression in another
- Cultural capital, which includes the knowledge and skills associated with understanding symbols, impacts individuals' social opportunities and inclusion in culture.
- Meanings of symbols can evolve and be interpreted differently based on individual experiences and societal contexts.

## Symbols as branding
- To "*brand*" a corporation is to create meaning from the symbols and is done by marketing communication
- The symbol of McDonald includes coded information about the restaurant layout, staff behaviour, the menu etc.
- The Red Cross symbol represents neutrality, protection and humanitarian aid, creating hope and comfort to people

Differentiation -> Positioning -> Branding (*promotion*)

### Cultural failures in germany
>[!example]+
>**Clairol's Mist Stick**
>- When Clairol launched its "*Mist stick*" curling iron in Germany, the company apparently had no idea that "*Mist*" was a slang term for shit. The company discovered that few women were crying out for a manure stick
>
>**Vicks**
>- Vapour-rub manufacturer Vicks failed to attract much custom for its products in Germany. The problem was that "*V*" is pronounced as an "*F*" in German, meaning Vicks sounds like the German equivalent of the F-word
