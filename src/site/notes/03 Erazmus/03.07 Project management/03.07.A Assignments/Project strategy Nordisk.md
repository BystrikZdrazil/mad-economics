# What is a project strategy?
- **“A direction in a project that contributes to success of the project in its environment”**
- Not just goals and plans — includes **methods, governance, guidelines, tools, and choices**
- Strategy is **dynamic**, can change throughout the project lifecycle
- Must consider **multiple stakeholders**, not only the parent company
- The project is a **temporary organization**, so its environment includes the parent company as an _external_ actor

# The four project strategies

## Obedient Servant
- Parent company = dominant stakeholder
- Low independence
- Project’s purpose is to **fulfil the parent’s will**
- Success = how well the project supports the parent’s business strategy

## Independent Innovator
- High independence
- Encourages **innovation and new solutions**
- Project may **renew or change** the parent company’s strategy
- Success = degree of newness, impact, and strategic renewal

## Flexible Mediator
- Several strong stakeholders with different interests
- Project adapts and balances between them
- Success = **synergy** and alignment among stakeholders

## Strong Leader
- High independence + many stakeholders
- Creates its own strong internal culture and goals
- Success = internal capability + **impact on society/environment**

# Main points from the article
- Project strategy is **broader than goals and plans**
- Projects must position themselves in their **environment**, not only follow the parent company
- Strategy can be **static or dynamic** and may change during the project lifecycle
- Projects may have **multiple strong stakeholders**, not just one parent
- Projects are **temporary organizations** with their own strategic space
- Four strategy types depend on:
    - **Degree of independence**
    - **Number of strong stakeholders**
- Success criteria differ between stakeholders and may conflict

# Recommended project strategy for Novo Nordisk

## Recommended: Strong Leader
- Novo Nordisk operates in a **complex environment**: regulators, governments, patients, suppliers, investors
- Many strong stakeholders → fits the “many stakeholders” dimension
- Their projects (eg, drug development, sustainability initiatives) require **high independence** and long-term vision
- Success for Novo is measured not only financially but also by **societal impact** (public health, sustainability)
- Matches the Strong Leader definition:
    - Independent culture
    - Own goals
    - Societal/environmental impact as ultimate success

## Optional alternative (if needed): Independent Innovator
- Could fit R&D projects focused on innovation and strategic renewal
