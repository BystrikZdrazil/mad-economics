# Definition
- Risk refers to uncertainty and severity of events and consequences affecting something humans value

>[!quote]+
> “Risk refers to uncertainty about and severity of the events and consequences (or outcomes) of an activity or project with respect to something that humans value” (Aven & Renn, 2009)

- Risk analysis identifies and manages potential problems that could undermine business initiatives

- A concrete assessment of potential risks and consequences affecting the business directly or indirectly
 

# Purpose
- Helps project managers identify what can go wrong
- Supports creation of prevention plans
- Increases awareness and preparedness
- Enables prioritization of risks

# Examples of Risks
- Financial risk
- Employee leaving the team
- Psychological risk (rumors of change)
- Corona
- Political risks

# Risk Analysis Process
- **1. Risk identification**
    - Brainstorming what can go wrong
- **2. Consequence evaluation**
    - Assessing impact on time, quality, expenses, staff/resources
- **3. Probability assessment**
    - Probability rated on a scale from 1 to 10
- **4. Overall risk calculation**
    - Consequence × probability
    - Used to prioritize risks
- **5. Action plan**
    - Prevention
    - Reduction
    - Transfer
    - Acceptance
    - Precautions
- **6. Monitoring**
    - Evaluate effectiveness of plans
    - Monitor progression
    - Identify changes needed

# Risk Control Plan (Table Structure)
- Risk
- Indicator
- Responsible
- What has been done
- Needs to be done further
- Deadline
- Last updated
