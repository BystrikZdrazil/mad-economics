# How it works
- They have shown interest in us (*permission-based marketing*)
- We create and send relevant content to subscribers
- Email does NOT sell the product
- It drives traffic to a landing page
- The landing page does the selling
- Goal: Get the click (*CTR*), not the sale

# Types of e-mails
**Promotions**: Boost sales and special offers through targeted campaigns for specific segments.  

**Newsletters**: Build loyalty, nurture leads, and educate audiences with regular value-based content. 

**Lifecycle Drips**: Guide customers from acquisition to onboarding, retention, and loyalty.  

**Transactional Emails**: Confirmation, shipping updates, and receipts keep customers informed and enhance trust.  

**Surveys and Feedback**: Gather insights and strengthen your community by inviting direct input.

# Types of e-mail lists
**Best results**: Your own opt-in list (*house list*)

**Second best**: Affiliate lists

**Worst**: rented lists (*violate GDPR in the EU*)

**Why?**
- Trust (know, like, trust principle)
- Permission marketing
- Higher engagement and conversion

# How to get subscribers
Offer a free value in exchange for sending them e-mails.

**B2B primarily**
- Newsletter
- PDF /guide
- Free valuable content

**B2C primarily**
- Discounts
- VIP-Access to sales/new products
- Free valuable content

# Sales VS content of your e-mails
**Types of e-mails**
- 50%+ content
- Max 50% sales

**Too many sales emails**
- Opt out (*unsubscribing*)
- Lower engagement

>[!tip]+
>Add value before selling

# Writing an e-mail copy
**Goal**: Get them to open
- Create curiosity
- Personalization works
- A/B test subject lines
- Small changes = big differences in CTR

**Content focusing on**
- Problem: “Worried about…?”
- Benefit: “Download this free guide…”
- Curiosity: “50% off this week only”

>[!tip]+
>Sell the **click**, not the **product**

## CTA (*call to actions*)
- Include 2–3 links per e-mail
- Make clicking **easy** and **obvious**

**Placement**
- Early
- End
- P.S.

**Format**
- Single-topic (continuous narrative)
- Multi-topic (segmented narrative)
- Mix HTLM/Text
- Focus on designing for “scanners”

**Length**: 300–700 words

# How to avoid the spam folder
**List quality**
- Use opt-in lists only
- Remove inactive subscribers

**Content & subject lines**
- Send from trusted platforms
- Avoid ALL CAPS, spam words, and “*!!!*”
- Write clear, honest, value-driven content
- Match subject line with email content

**Design & frequency**
- Balance text and images
- Optimize for mobile
- Send consistently (*not too often*)
- Clear unsubscribe link

# 5 central e-mail flows
1. Welcome
2. Abandoned cart
3. Browse abandonment
4. Post-purchase
5. Win-back

## Welcome
**Why it’s important**
- It’s the beginning of a long ”relationship” with the customer
- Make it clear that have signed up
- High engagement at this point
- Why are you unique

**Best practice**
- 4-6 e-mails
- Persuasion = trust and conversion

## Abandoned cart
**Why it’s important**
- High purchase intent
- Recovers lost revenue

**Best practice**
- 2–3 e-mails
- Clear CTA
- Product reminder

## Browse abandonment
**Why it’s important**
- Low-medium purchase intent
- Maybe they have looked at it before?

**Best practice**
- 1–3 emails
- Product reminder: selling fast

## Post purchase
- To increase loyalty and repeat purchases.
- Order confirmation
- Product guidance
- Upsell / cross‑sell
- Request for a review

**Best practice**
- 5-7 e-mails

## Win back
Why it’s important
- Reactivates inactive subscribers
- Recovers lost revenue
- Improves email deliverability
- Reminder (*“We miss you”*)
- Final opt-out / re-engagement
- Value (*tips, inspiration*)
- Incentive (*discount / offer*)

**Best practice**
- 3–4 e-mails
- Mix of value + incentive
- Clear CTA in every email

# Measure your success
**Bounce Rate**
- % of emails not delivered

**Opt-out Rate**
- % who unsubscribe

**Open Rate**
- % who open the email

**Click-through Rate (*CTR*)**
- % who click the link

**Conversion Rate**
- % who complete desired action

**Gross Revenue**
- Final sales generated driven by:
	- List size
	- Price
	- CTR
	- Conversion rate
