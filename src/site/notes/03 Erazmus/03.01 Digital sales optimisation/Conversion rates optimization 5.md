Conversion rates are often used as a KPI to review the effectiveness of e-commerce sites.

CRO is the process of adjusting the website and content to achieve higher conversion of visitors.

Just because you have attracted an audience doesn't mean they are ready to buy. Only a small percentage of people who land on a site will go through with a transaction whether it's completing a transaction or signing up for a newsletter.

A conversion happens when we turn one thing into another. It refers to people who engage in a specific desired activity that furthers your business goals.

>[!example]+
>- Curious readers into avid subscribers
>- Digital window shoppers into paying customers
>- Early-stage buyers into account holders

CRO contains a number of different elements that need to be put into play (*website, landing page, blogs, pricing page, page layout, CTA, headlines*) which makes the task complex.

>[!example]+
>Basekit's pricing page redesign
>- More prominent, neat and spaced-out design
>- Addition of colour to highlight the prices
>- "*Try now*" is what makes the new design more appealing and easier to follow
>
>These minor design changes helped Basekit to increase their conversion rate by 25%.
>

- Unbounce 2021 **Conversion Benchmark Report** compares landing page conversion rates for 16 different sectors
- **Conversion Rate** = **# Sales** / **# Visits**
- According to psychology, a brand has about 1/10 second to make a first impression
- That’s why a 1-second page delay can decrease conversion rates by up to 7%, or why simply changing the colour of a button can increase conversion rates by 21%

# [[03 Erazmus/03.01 Digital sales optimisation/SEO basics\|SEO]] vs CRO
- SEO helps people find your website.
- CRO helps more of those visitors take action once they arrive.

Both aim to improve business results online, but they focus on different parts of the customer journey.

- SEO is the process of optimizing your position in Google
- SEO focuses on things like keywords, metadata, content structure and link-worthiness to boost site traffic
- SEO and CRO do have things in common. **Focus on both** as they work well together. For both you will focus on:
	- **Well-written**, (*expert*) content that both elevates your standing in Google and improves conversion rates.
	- **Optimized headlines**, meta descriptions, and technical SEO to entice potential customers and improve the user experience.
	- **Page layout**: Well-designed pages are helpful from more than just a human perspective. They also help Google index your page and provide the most useful featured snippets.

## Main goals of [[03 Erazmus/03.01 Digital sales optimisation/SEO basics\|SEO]]
- Improve rankings in search engines.
- Increase organic website traffic.
- Attract relevant visitors.
- Build long-term online visibility.
- Reduce dependence on paid advertising.

## Main goals of CRO
- Increase the percentage of visitors who convert.
- Improve the user experience.
- Reduce friction in the customer journey.
- Increase revenue, leads, or sign-ups.
- Make marketing traffic more profitable.

# Landing page
A landing page is a standalone web page designed specifically for a marketing campaign, aimed at driving a single, focused action (*conversion*).

While a homepage typically has many navigation options and aims for general exploration, landing pages are designed with a single focus  

A good landing page is crucial for converting the visitor into a sale. That’s why it is important to keep conversion in mind when creating a landing page.

Without a landing page, it's difficult to collect information about people who visit your website, making it difficult to understand them, market to them, and ultimately convert  

A good landing page targets a specific group, such as traffic from an email campaign promoting a specific eBook or visitors clicking on a pay-per-click ad  

It's important to create a unique landing page for each of the offers you create.  

For example, allows visitors to download content (*e-books, white papers, videos, webinars, etc.*) or sign up for offers like free samples or demos of your product

>[!tip]+
>- Customers expect brands to deliver content to them in an engaging and entertaining way.
>- Video is ‘the currency’ of the Internet.
>- 90% say that product videos help them make purchasing decisions.
>- 64% are more likely to buy after watching a video.
>- 97% of marketers say video helps users understand their product or service
