# The role of CRM
CRM connects [[03 Erazmus/03.01 Digital sales optimisation/Inbound marketing\|inbound]] to sales by:
- Storing customer interactions
- Tracking behaviour and intent
- Aligning marketing and sales efforts

It captures and structures customer relationships.

## CRM, automation and revenue modeling
How do businesses connect customer data, lead management, marketing automation, funnel metrics, and financial impact, in an effective, timely and seamless manner?

**CRM basics**: Customer data as the commercial memory of the company

**[[03 Erazmus/03.01 Digital sales optimisation/Lead generation\|Lead management]]**: Visitor -> lead -> MQL -> SQL ->> opportunity -> customer

## CRM system
They store customer information, track interactions, manage leads, and support sales/marketing coordination. CRM is often the core part of the [[03 Erazmus/03.01 Digital sales optimisation/MarTech\|Martech]] stack alongside automation, analytics, CMS, adtech and social/content tools.

|**CRM object**|**Meaning**|
|---|---|
|Contact|Individual person|
|Company/account|Organization the person belongs to|
|Lead|Potential customer not yet sales-ready|
|Deal/opportunity|Active sales possibility|
|Activity|Email, call, meeting, form submission, website visit|
|Pipeline stage|Where the prospect is in the buying process|

- CRM connects **marketing**, **sales**, and **customer service**.
- CRM (*and Automation elements*) are normally tied to understanding sales/marketing technology, analysing lead flow and sales pipelines, and strengthening marketing-sales alignment.

## [[03 Erazmus/03.01 Digital sales optimisation/Lead generation\|Lead]] management
- Visitor -> Lead -> MQL -> SQL -> Opportunity -> Customer

Companies should define a revenue model with clear stages, rules for movement between stages, and agreement between marketing and sales about handoff points.

It also distinguished TOFU, MOFU, and BOFU, with leads progressing from awareness/new names through MQL, SQL, opportunity, and customer

### How lead scoring works
- 96% of the visitors to a website will not make a purchase right away
- Lead scoring sorts visitors and prospects and identifies those most likely to purchase in the future

To create a lead scoring model, you assign **value** to consumer actions and attributes that rank those leads according to their likelihood of buying a product.

When prioritizing the leads ready to buy, **you can spend extra resources** on lead nurturing for high-value prospects and increase your chances of a conversion for a greater ROI.

- **Fit score**: Is this the right type of customer?
- **Behaviour score**: Are they acting like they are interested?
- **Intent score**: Are they showing buying signals?

>[!example]+
>| **Action**                                        | **Points** |
>| --------------------------------------------- | ------ |
>| Downloads guide                               | +10    |
>| Opens email                                   | +2     |
>| Visits pricing page                           | +20    |
>| Attends webinar                               | +25    |
>| Student/private email instead of company mail | -5     |
>| Unsubscribes                                  | -30    |

![DSO_lead_scoring.png](/img/user/06%20Images/DSO_lead_scoring.png)

Lead scoring assigns value to every visitor action and attributes to rank them according to their likelihood of conversion.
- **Fit** = how well they match your buyer persona.
- **Interest** = how likely they are to buy based on their behavior

# Revenue modeling
*How many customers and how much profit does the funnel create?*

## Making marketing financially visible

| **Stage** | **Volume** | **Conversion rate** | **Output**       |
| --------- | ---------- | ------------------- | ---------------- |
| *Visitors*  | 10 000     | 5%                  | 500 Leads        |
| *Leads*     | 500        | 30%                 | 150 MQLs         |
| *MQLs*      | 150        | 50%                 | 75 SQLs          |
| *SQLs*      | 75         | 40%                 | 30 Opportunities |
| *Opps*      | 30         | 25%                 | 7.5 Customers    |


**Financial impact**
7.5 customers * 2000€ = 15000€ revenue

40% margin = 6000€ GP - 3000€ campaign cost = 3000€ profit

## Automation requires judgement
The technical question "*Can we automate?*", the marketing question is "*Should we?*"
- **Data quality**
	- Wring data creates wrong action
- **Over-automation**
	- Too many messages damage trust
- **Privacy VS personalization**
	- Relevance can become intrusive
- **Consent & GDPR**
	- Permission, transparency and minimization
- **Bias in scoring**
	- Rules may favour some agreements unfairly
