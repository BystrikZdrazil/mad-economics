How can companies structure digital sales using different business models and sales strategies?

**Core digital business models, which are often combined**
- E-commerce
- Omnichannel
- Subscription-based models
- Marketplaces

For **digital marketeres**, the business model determines:
- Which channels matter most
- How customer data is collected and used
- Which conversion points should be optimized
- Which metrics define success in the short and long term

>[!important]+
>How does marketing activity create **measurable customer value and revenue**?

# E-commerce
They have different approaches to digital sales, but in practice they often overlap.
- E-commerce means selling products or services primarily through digital channels, such as a webshop app, social media, etc.

>[!example]+
>- Zalando
>- Amazon
>- ASOS
>- Coolshop

**Platforms**
- Shopify
- Hubspot
- Magneto
- Adobe Commerce
- NetSuite

**Strategic elements**
- User experience and checkout optimization
- [[03 Erazmus/03.01 Digital sales optimisation/On-Page SEO\|SEO]], PPC, social media and [[03 Erazmus/03.01 Digital sales optimisation/E-mail marketing\|E-mail marketing]]
- Logistics, inventory and delivery experience
- Customer service through chatbots, AI and automation
- Conversion rate optimization and basket-size growth

**Advantages**
- Lower fixed costs than physical stores
- Potential global reach
- Easy to analyse customer behaviour through data

**Challenges**
- High competition and price transparency
- Requires strong digital marketing execution
- Returns, fulfilment and logistics can be complex

## Omnichannel strategies
It is an integrated strategy that combines physical and digital channels to create a seamless customer experience across touchpoints

>[!example]+
>**Examples of omnichannel activity**
>- **IKEA**: Customers start online and complete the purchase in-store, for example through click & collect
>- **H&M**: A customer buys online and exchanges the item in a physical store
>- **Apple**: Physical stores, online ordering and service are integrated

**Key elements**:
- Consistent customer experience across mobile, desktop and physical stores
- Data synchronization between CRM, marketing platforms and inventory systems
- Personalization and AI-driven recommendations
- Clear attribution across touchpoints

**Advantages**:
- Higher customer satisfaction and loyalty
- Better customer insight across channels
- Higher chance of conversion

**Challenges**:
- Requires investment in technology and system integration
- Creates logistical complexity in inventory and order synchronization

# Subscription-based models
They are popular because customers pay a recurring monthly or annual fee for access to a product or service.

## SaaS (Software as a Service)
**Advantages**
- Predictable revenue
- Scalability
- Lower entry barriers
- 
**Challenges**
- Churn
- Retention pressure
- Continuous product development

>[!example]+
>- Spotify
>- Netflix
>- Adobe creative cloud
>- Microsoft 365

## Membership services
**Advantages**
- Loyalty
- Recurring engagegment
- Perceived exclusivity

**Challenges**
- Subscription fatigue
- High expectations for ongoing value

## Freemium model
- A free basic version with paid upgrades

>[!example]+
>- Dropbox
>- LinkedIn
>- Evernote

**Advantages**
- Large user base
- Low barrier to trial
- Data about user behaviour

**Challenges**
- Only a small percentage convert to paying customers
- Free users can increase costs
- Requires careful onboarding and upgrade triggers

# Marketplace strategies
A marketplace model means selling through third-party platforms instead of building and operating a fully independent webshop.

## Types of marketplaces
**B2C marketplaces** (*Amazon, eBay, Zalando*)
**B2B marketplaces** (*Alibaba, ThomasNet*)
**Peer-to-peer marketplaces** (*Etsy, Airbnb, Facebook marketplace*)

Self owned e-commerce platforms, such as Shopify, allow businesses to create their own online store while using platform tools for payments, fulfilment and marketing.

>[!important]+
>**Marketplace**: Access to demand, but less control
>**Own webshop**: More control, but harder to build traffic

**Advantages**:
- Large customer base from day one
- Lower technical barriers
- Higher visibility and platform trust

**Challenges**:
- Strong competition
- Platform commissions and rules
- Limited control over branding, customer data and customer relationship

# Data-driven sales strategies
Data plays a central role in digital sales because companies can use customer data and behavioural analytics to predict buying patterns and optimize marketing through personalization.

## Important elements
- Use AI and machine learning to predict customer behaviour
- Create personalized offers and product recommendations
- Use dynamic pricing based on demand, timing or behaviour
- Use retargeting and remarketing to reconnect with previous visitors
- Build customer segments based on needs, value and behaviour
- **Digital marketing focus**: data should improve relevance, not just increase message volume.

**Advantages**:
- Higher conversion rates through more relevant offers
- Better customer experience and engagement
- Higher customer lifetime value (CLV)

**Challenges**:
- GDPR, consent and data security must be respected
- Requires analytics skills and good data quality
- Poor personalization can feel intrusive or irrelevant

# Performance marketing VS branding
Digital marketers need both short-term conversion metrics and long-term brand metrics.

## Performance marketing
- Shorter term value delivery
- **Goal**: Direct and measurable results such as sales, leads or sign-ups
- **Typical channels**: PPC, paid social, affiliate marketing, email marketing and retargeting
- **Typical metrics**: ROOI, ROAS, CAC, [[03 Erazmus/03.01 Digital sales optimisation/Customer lifetime value\|CLV]]

**Advantages**:
- Fast feedback and measurable results
- Easy to test and optimize

**Limitations**:
- Can become dependent on paid traffic
- Can be expensive without organic or brand support

## Branding
- Longer-term value creation
- **Goal**: build brand identity, trust, preference and loyalty.
- **Typical activities**: content marketing, storytelling, influencer marketing, community building and customer experience.
- **Typical metrics**: awareness, consideration, NPS, loyalty and share of search.

**Advantages**:
- Builds long-term value and demand
- Reduces dependence on paid media over time

**Limitations**:
- Takes time to show financial impact
- Harder to measure precisely

# BMC environment model
A business model is not created in a vacuum.

Organizations must respond to changing market conditions, technological development and shifting consumer preferences.

For digital marketers, this means that channel choices, content formats, pricing, data use and customer journeys must be reviewed continuously.

To succeed, companies need business models that are flexible, innovative and able to adapt to the complexity of the modern digital business ecosystem.
