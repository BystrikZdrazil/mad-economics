# Churn
- In case of cancelling a subscription → give them a discount offer to keep them/lure them back
- One-time to occasional buy → retain customers by providing periodical comparisons of product version improvement
- Occasional buy → loyalty rewarding system/program + point-out the quality + point-out the performance
    - Highlight the product leadership
    - Important to keep the quality statement as honest as possible (since occasional buy allows customer evaluation on a more regular basis)

# CLV - Customer Lifetime Value
- Subscription → provide more ‘perks’ to come with the subscription (netflix + games, apple with apple music/tv)
- One-time to occasional buy → iterate between 2 qualities that make it seem like your product is improving at a much greater rate than it actually is (gorilla glass, one year improving scratch-resistance and the other improving drop resistance, even though these 2 qualities are countered by each other)
- Occasional buy → every once in a while highlight the product’s superiority to competitors, to keep the customers using it longer (toothpaste - “Our toothpaste is the best whitening product on the market right now”)
