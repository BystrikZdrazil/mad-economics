Digital business models are how companies create, deliver and capture value with digital technologies.
- How do digital businesses operate, and how an they implement and optimize sales & marketing strategies

There is no single universally accepted definition of a business model. For now we use it as:
- A holistic view of how a company does business
- A summary of how a company creates, delivers and captures value
- A model made up of several connected elements that can be analysed in more detail

[[03 Erazmus/03.01 Digital sales optimisation/Business model canvas\|Business Model Canvas]] (BMC) is a widely used tool because it offers an  
overview of the company’s:
1. Value proposition
2. Target groups
3. Infrastructure
4. Financial logic

For digital marketers, business models are dynamic: they develop continuously in interaction with customers, platforms, suppliers, investors and competitors.

# Specific aspects
- Marketing is no longer just promotion
	- Digital value is built into
		- Products
		- Services
		- Platforms
		- Data
		- Pricing

# Bock and Wiener's taxonomy
Bock and Wiener’s taxonomy helps us analyse how data and digital technologies are used in business models

It distinguishes five broad dimensions: digital offerings and experiences, digital platforms, data analytics and digital pricing. Each dimension can appear in different forms depending on the company and market.

NB: use the taxonomy as a checklist. Not every dimension needs to be present in every digital business model.

The taxonomy helps identify where digital technology creates value
- What is offered digitally?
- How is the offering delivered or experienced?
- Who participates in the platform or ecosystem?
- What data is collected and analysed?
- How do companies capture revenue through pricing & monetization?
- Not all dimensions need to be present in the same business model
