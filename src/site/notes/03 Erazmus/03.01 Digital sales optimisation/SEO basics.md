- Search Engine Optimisation
- Organic (*non-paid*) search results
- Attracts **relevant, high-intent traffic**
- Converts visitors into **leads and customers**
- Hard work = high reward
- You have to know the algorithm

**SEO supports**
- Online visibility
- Trust
- Long-term sales growth

# Why it matters
- More than 90% of purchases online start with a google search
- Organic traffic = more trust than ads
- SEO reduces dependency on paid media
- Strong SEO = lower cost per acquisition (*CPA*)

# How search engine works
- **Crawling** - bots scan websites
- **Indexing** - content is stored and categorized
- **Ranking** - results are ranked on quality

## Crawling
You can think of crawling as Google discovering you page. The bots scan your site and "*read*" it like a browser does.

**What’s important SEO wise**
- Pages must be accessible for bots
- Links help bots discover content
- Clean structure matters

## Indexing
After a page is crawled, Google tries to understand what it is about and categorize it.
- Text, headings, images, and structure are analyzed
- The page is stored in Google's massive database (*the index*)
- Google decides which topics and keywords the page belongs to

**What’s important SEO wise**
- Clear headings (H1, H2)
- Relevant keywords
- Well‑structured content

## Ranking
When a user searches for something.

Google ranks pages based on signals like:
- Relevance to the query
- Content quality
- Authority and trust
- User experience

**What's important SEO-wise**
- Better matching intent = higher ranking
- Helpful content beats keyword stuffing
- Quality > quantity

# Types of search intent
1. Informational
	- "*What is SEO?*"
2. Navigational
	- "*Google analytics login*"
3. Commercial
	- "*Best SEO tools*"
4. Transactional
	- "*Buy SEO software*"

# Keywords
A keyword is what users type into search engines.
- **Short-tail** - "*SEO*"
- **Long-tail** - "*SEO for ecommerce websites*"

Long-tail keywords benefits
- Less competition
- Higher conversion rate
- More specific intent
