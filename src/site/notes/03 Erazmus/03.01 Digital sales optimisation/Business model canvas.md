The **value proposition canvas** is a strategic management framework that visually maps how a product or service creates genuine value for a target customer.

The Value Proposition Canvas helps marketers answer three essential questions:
- Who exactly are we creating value for?
- What problems, needs, desires, or frustrations do they have?
- How does our product, service, campaign, or digital experience create value for them?

For digital marketers, this tool is useful when designing:
- Landing pages
- Paid ads
- E-mail campaigns
- Social media content
- Customer journeys
- E-commerce experiences
- App onboarding flows
- Lead-generation offers
- Brand positioning

>[!example]+
>**Duolingo**
>![DSO_duolingo_VPC.png](/img/user/06%20Images/DSO_duolingo_VPC.png)
>
>Duolingo’s core value proposition is to make language and skill learning free, accessible, and addictive
>
>Chosen customer segment:
>- Busy students or young adults who want to learn a new language in a flexible, low-pressure way.
>- This segment is important because Duolingo is not only competing with language schools.
>- It also competes with YouTube, textbooks, private tutors, apps, and the simple choice of doing nothing.
>
>|**Type**|**Customer jobs**|
>|---|---|
>|*Functional jobs*|Learn a new language step by step|
>|*Functional jobs*|Practice vocabulary, grammar, listening, and pronunciation|
>|*Functional jobs*|Prepare for travel, study abroad, work, or exams|
>|*Functional jobs*|Build a daily learning habit|
>|*Functional jobs*|Learn without committing to expensive classes|
>|*Emotional jobs*|Feel that language learning is achievable|
>|*Emotional jobs*|Feel motivated and rewarded|
>|*Social jobs*|Be able to communicate with people from other cultures|
>|*Social jobs*|Feel smart, international, or self-improving|
>
>**Customer pains**: What frustrates/worries the customer
>- Language learning feels difficult or overwhelming
>- Traditional courses can be expensive
>- Classes require fixed schedules
>- It is hard to stay motivated over time
>- Students may feel embarrassed speaking a new language
>- Progress can feel slow
>- Grammar explanations can feel boring
>- It is easy to forget vocabulary
>- People often quit after a few days or weeks
>- Learners may not know where to start
>
>**Customer gains**: What the customer wants to achieve or experience
>- Learn in small, manageable steps
>- Practice anytime and anywhere
>- Feel daily progress
>- Stay motivated through streaks, levels, and rewards
>- Learn for free or at a low cost
>- Feel confident using basic phrases
>- Prepare for real-life situations, such as travel
>- Make learning feel fun instead of stressful
>- Build a habit without needing much time
>- Compare progress with friends or other learners
>
>Possible **value proposition statement**
>"*Duolingo helps busy students and young adults learn a new language in small, fun, daily lessons - anytime, anywhere, and for free.*"
