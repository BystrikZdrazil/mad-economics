# Consumer behaviour framework
- Funnel thinking is a strategic framework that maps the customer path from initial brand awareness to final purchase and loyalty.
- By dividing this into stages
	- **Awareness** (*TOFU*)
	- **Consideration** (*MOFU*)
	- **Conversion** (*BOFU*)
- Marketers can tailor content and tactics to specific audience needs, optimizing for higher conversion rates

# Customer journey
A customer journey map shows the steps and touchpoints on the ‘path-to-purchase’ from customer awareness to product comparison, selection and purchase.

They are often used as part of customer persona analysis and the buying process of a persona.

It is a non-linear, multi-touchpoint, omni channel path.

The are thousands of visualized customer journeys available focusing on various variables and elements.

To improve results for any aspect of any business, performance management is vital

Successful companies prioritize resources to monitoring online activities, develop processes to continuously improve the performance of digital channels.

# Why?
Organizations are constantly looking to get better at evaluating and measuring (*employee*) performance. Performance management is aimed at improving an organization’s bottom line by stimulating employee performance.

It also aligns workforce and individual goals with the wider organizational goals and mission.

# How?
A performance management system is a mechanism for tracking the performance consistently.

It ensures that employees and departments across the organization are working effectively towards achieving the business’ strategic goals. Successful businesses gain an advantage from having employees better aligned with corporate goals.

# Benefits (*if done right*)
- Enable continuous performance management
- Offer 360-degree performance reviews
- Improve the frequency of feedback
- Ensure a fair performance appraisal process
- Make it easy to track (*employee*) performance
- Identify training requirements
- Automate performance review reporting

# Potential barriers
- PMS not seen as a priority, not understood or targeted at the wrong targets 
	- Reducing costs rather than improving performance
- **Unclear responsibilities** for delivering and improving measurement system
- **Resourcing issues** – lack of time (*lack of staff motivation?*), the necessary technology and integrated systems
- **Data problems** – data overlay or poor quality, limited data for benchmarking

# KPIs vs metrics
**KPIs** are tied to specific goals, **metrics** are data points

A **KPI** is a business outcome or a goal that a specific team or department is trying to achieve.

**Metrics** are different data points in our funnels or analytics platforms that, when accumulated, make up a KPI.

| **KPI**                                                                                                                               | **Metrics**                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Quantifiable value used to track progress against set goals (*individual, team, company*)                                             | Quantifiable measure used to track progress and evaluate success                                                                                                             |
| Provide direction towards achieving desired results and can help you business make better-informed decisions                          | Business metrics are used to track progress and performance in certain areas that are critical to the health of a business, such as revenue, customers, employees, and so on |
| A good KPI must be well-defined and measurable, relevant to your business and able to provide a clear picture of recorded performance | It's important not to confuse a metric with a measure too                                                                                                                    |
|                                                                                                                                       | While a measure is a fundamental or unit-specific term, a metric can be derived from one or more measures                                                                    |
## Metrics
1. Business performance metrics
2. Website metrics
3. AD/social media metrics

- Several ways of clustering metrics
	- Core marketing KPI's & metrics (*ROI, CAC, Conversion rate, CLTV, etc.*)
	- Traffic & engagement metrics (*organic traffic, click-through rate, bounce rate, engagement rate, etc.*)

Efficiency metrics (*Are we spending wisely?*)
Engagement metrics (*Do people care about us?*)
Growth metrics (*Are we expanding?*)

**Short-term: efficiency**
*Metrics*: CAC, CPC, ROI
*Focus*: Are we profitable enough?

**Mid-term: engagement**
*Metrics*: CTR, engagement rate, bounce rate
*Focus*: Are people interacting & building interest?

**Long-term: growth**
*Metrics*: conversion rate, CLV, organic traffic
*Focus*: Are we scaling sustainably?

>[!example]+
>- **Conversion Rate (*CVR*):** Measures the percentage of users who complete a desired action, such as filling out a form or buying a product.
>- **Customer Acquisition Cost (*CAC*):** The total cost (*advertising + team time*) to acquire one new customer.
>- **Return on Investment (*ROI*) & Return on Ad Spend (*ROAS*):** Measures revenue generated compared to the marketing budget or ad spend.
>- **Customer Lifetime Value (*CLV/LTV*):** Predicts the total revenue a business can expect from a single customer relationship.
>- **Cost per Lead (*CPL*):** Measures the cost-effectiveness of generating potential leads, crucial for lead generation campaigns.
>- **Click-Through Rate (*CTR*):** The ratio of users who click on a link (*email, ad, website*) to the total users who view it.
>- **Organic Traffic & Keyword Rankings:** Measures the visibility of a website on search engines and the quality of SEO efforts.
>- **Bounce Rate / Engagement Rate:** Indicates the percentage of users who leave a site after viewing only one page, signaling engagement levels.
>- **Social Media Engagement/Reach:** Tracks likes, shares, and comments to evaluate brand awareness and audience interaction.
>- **Marketing Qualified Leads (*MQLs*) to SQLs:** Tracks the number of leads generated and how many are accepted by sales, gauging lead quality.

### Key benefits of KPI dashboard
**Detailed dive**
KPI dashboards allow users to organize, filter, drill down, analyze and visualize their most important key targets
Helps translate large, complex data into an easy-to-understand format

**Discover changes or issues at scale**
An organized dashboard that displays key KPIs allows users to easily determine which outcomes are being met and what may demand priority

**Efficient decision-making**
KPI dashboards bring the most critical operational data of a business into one place, making KPIs more visible and increase efficiency in analyzing performance, setting objectives, etc.

**Increased collaboration**
A visual overview fosters better collaboration and communication among users, as everyone has access to the same dashboards to discuss performance and can come together to improve it.

**Real-time ROI analysis**
By monitoring the organization’s most important performance metrics in real-time, users can see which activities have worked and by how much, allowing for better financial outcomes long-term.

**Unearth new opportunities and trends**
KPI dashboards help users to spot patterns in the data and extract potential opportunities to define or refine the objectives and enhance and improve the overall business performance

>[!important]+
>According to [[03 Erazmus/03.11 Supply chain management/Gartner\|Gartner]], the best metrics:
>- Have a clearly defined and causal relationship to a business outcome
>- Work as a leading, not lagging, indicator
>- Address a specific, defined audience
>- Can be understood by a non-IT audience
>- Drive action when they change from green to yellow to red.
