# Key elements
1. Digital products or services
	- It can be fully digital (*apps, streaming, SaaS*) or digitally enhanced (*online banking, e-commerce*)
2. Use of data
	1. Digital businesses rely heavily on data to make decisions, personalize experiences, and optimize operations
3. Digital channels
	1. They interact with customers through websites, mobile apps, social media, and online platforms
4. Technology driven processes
	1. Automation, cloud computing, AI, and software systems are used to run and scale the business efficiently
5. New business models
	1. Subscription (*Netflix*)
	2. Platform based (*Uber*)
	3. E-commerce (*Amazon*)

# Clarification of concepts

| **Digitization**                                | **Digitalization**                                                                                     | **Digital Transformation**                                               |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Conversion of information into the digital form | Integratino of digital information into operational processes, business models, services, and products | Overall process of digitalization across the while business/organization |

- **2001** - 3rd generation mobile phone with digital texting and sign feature sharing
- **2006** - facebook as major digital social media launched crossing 10 million users in a decade
- **2007** - Apple launched the iPhone
- **2016** - Digital manufacturing touch new high as India launch cheapest smart phone, mobile subscription crossing 7BN

# Waves of digital disruption
- **1995** - Music, photography, video rental
- **2010** - Print media, TV, travel, HR
- **2015** - Finance, healthcare, automotive, retails
- **2020** - All safe havens will be subject to digital disruption

Digital disruption is **radical changes** caused by **digital technologies** and **business models** that transforms or relaces established industry norms, products or services.

It alters **customer behaviour**, forces **reevaluation** of **value propositions**, and enables faster more **efficient alternatives** to traditional, often analog, industries

# Digital business in historical context
- Digital transformation of business (*1995 - 2025*)
- It is not only about technology, it also is about
	- Lowering **transaction costs**
	- Scaling at **near-zero marginal cost**
	- Turning **data** into **competitive advantage**

## Internet & Dot-Com Era
- Mid 1990s - early 2000s
- **Key inventions**
	- The World Wide Web
	- Web browsers (*Netscape, Internet Explorer*)
	- Search engines
	- E-commerce infrastructure
- **Key players**
	- Amazon (*1994*)
		- Relentless focus on customer experience
		- Built logistics + infrastructure early
		- Willingness to lose money short-term for long-term dominance
	- eBay (*1995*)
	- Google (*1998*)
		- **Breakthrough**: PageRank algorithm
		- **Monetization**: targeted ads (*AdWords*)
		- **Simplicity** + **speed** -> user lock in
	- Microsoft (*1975*)
		- Controlled the operating system war (*Windows*)
		- Leveraged distribution power (*pre-installation*)

## Platforms & Web 2.0
- 2003 - 2010
- **Key inventions**
	- User-generated content
	- Social networks
	- APIs (*Application programming interface*) platform ecosystems
- **Key players**
	- Facebook (*meta*)
		- Strong network effects (*value grows with users*)
		- Real identity -> high engagement
		- Monetizes attention via ads
	- YouTube (*acquired by google*)
	- Apple (*iPhone*)
		- Integrated hardware + software + ecosystem
		- Focus on design and UX
	- Salesforce (*cloud CRM pioneer*)
		- First major success of Software-as-a-Service (*SaaS*)
		- Eliminated need for on-premise IT

## Mobile, cloud & data
- 2010 - 2018
- **Key inventions**
	- Smartphones at scale
	- Cloud computing (*Azure, AWS*)
	- Big data analytics
	- App economy
- **Key players**
	- Amazon (*AWS*)
		- Turned internal infrastructure into a global service
		- **First mover** -> massive scale advantage
		- **Pay-as-you-go** pricing model
	- Apple & Google (*mobile duopoly, iOS/Android*)
	- Uber/Airbnb
		- **Asset-light** platforms
		- Match supply and demand efficiently
		- Scale globally fast via apps
	- Netflix
		- Shift from DVD to streaming
		- Heavy use of **algorithms** for **personalization**

> [!tip]
> Data + scale + platform = **exponential advantage**

# Cross-cutting patterns
- **Critical for success**

1. **Network effects**
	- Facebook, Uber, Airbnb
	- More users -> more value -> winner takes all/most
2. **Economies of scale**
	- Amazon, Google, Microsoft, Netflix
	- High fixed cost, near-zero marginal cost
3. **Ecosystem control**
	- Apple (*AppStore*)
	- Microsoft (*enterprise stack*)
	- Google (*search + ads + android*)
4. **Data advantage**
	- Netflix, Google, Amazon
	- Better data -> better decisions -> better products
5. **Platform strategy**
	- Shift from selling products -> enabling others to build on you

# Why some failed
- **Nokia**
	- Missed smartphones
- **Blockbuster**
	- Ignored streaming
- **Yahoo**
	- Failed to focus

## Core reasons
- Ignored **technological shifts**
- Failed to adapt business models
- Underestimated **platform dynamics**
