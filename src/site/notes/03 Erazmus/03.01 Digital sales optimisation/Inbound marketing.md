- Customer-centric digital marketing approach
- Launched by HubSpot in 2006
- Attracting customers "*naturally*"
- Valuable, relevant and personalized content
- Permission based (*e-mail, cookies,...*)
- Earns attention instead of buying it

- It is ongoing
- Behavior‑driven
- Data-based
- Focused on lifetime value

The right mindset for inbound marketing is to shift from selling to helping

| **Inbound marketing**      | **Outbound marketing** |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Pulls customers in         | Pushes messages        |
| Helps users solve problems | Interrupts users       |
| Builds long-term trust     | Short-term attention   |

# Why traditional marketing is less effective
- Banner blindness
- AD fatigue
- Spam filters and AD blockers
- Customers avoid interruptions

# The modern consumer
- Research independently (*online*)
- Compare options digitally (*pricerunner, reviews*)
- Expect personalization
- Engage on multiple channels

**Today:** Sales influence starts before contact with the consumer.

**Rule of seven:** on average, customers needs to encounter a brand/business 7 times before they make a purchase.

# Why is the fly wheel effective
It moves away from the traditional marketing funnel and focuses on repeating the loop

## Attract (*the right audience*)
- Only relevant potential customers
- Focus on quality, not traffic volume

### How
- SEO-optimized blog content
- Educational guides and videos
- Social media content
- UX and discovery optimization

### Benefits
- More intentional traffic
- Better lead quality
- Lower cost per acquisition

## Engage (*tools and methods*)
- Build relationships
- Understand needs and pain points
- Guide users toward solutions (*your product or service*)

### How
- E-mail marketing
- Marketing automation
- CRM data
- Lead nurturing workflows
- Chat and conversational tools (*AI*)

### When it works
- When it is timely and relevant
- Based on behaviour
- Adapted to buyer stage
- Personalization increases conversion rates

### Benefits
- Shorter sales cycles
- More informed prospects
- Better handover to sales
- Higher closing rates on sales

## Delight (*after the sales*)
- Ensure customer success
- Create positive experiences
- Turn customers into promoters
- Sales continue after purchase

### Key activities
- Support and service content
- Follow-up communication
- Loyalty and retention initiatives

### Benefits
- Buy again
- Recommend the brand
- Reduce acquisition costs
- Strengthen long-term revenue and relationship

# Loop marketing: express
- Clearly communicate value
- Strong value propositions
- Consistent messaging across channels

## How
- Brand identity, tone and consumer POV
- AI analyses high-value customers
- Style guide for your content

# Loop marketing: tailor
- Use data and CRM insignts
- Personalize experiences (*e-mails*)
- Adopt content and offers

# Loop marketing: amplify
- Extend the reach across platforms (*SoMe*)
- Reuse and scale content
- Use automation to expand impact

# Loop marketing: evolve
- Analyse behaviour and performance
- Optimize continuously
- Improve conversion and retention

# Why is inbound marketing important
- Buyers are influenced early
- Data improves decision-making
- Relevance increases conversion
- Optimization never stops

# KPIs in inbound sales optimization
- Lead quality
- Conversion rate
- Shorter sales cycle
- Customer lifetime value
- Higher retention rate
