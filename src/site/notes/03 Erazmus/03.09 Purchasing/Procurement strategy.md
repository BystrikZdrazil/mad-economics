# Procurement strategic process
Procurement strategic process and differentiated purchasing factors involves a lot of different factors
- SCM
- Supplier strategi
- Outsourcing/insourcing
- Buying situation etc.

Most procurement processes follow a similar cycle:
- Identify the need
- Define specifications and requirements
- Select the procurement type and method
- Source and evaluate potential suppliers
- Negotiate contracts and pricing
- Purchase, receive, and manage performance

**Procurement role** = company delivery service

The role of the purchasing department differs in different ways and depends on who is responsible for the different elements

# Strategy definition and goals
A procurement strategy is a structured plan that an organization develops to guide its purchasing process in a way that aligns with its business needs.

The goal of a procurement strategy is to ensure the efficient, cost-effective and sustainable acquisition of goods or services that are essential for the organization's operations.

>[!example]+
>Examples of goals that are common to many procurement strategies
>- Increasing delivery efficiency
>- Minimizing errors
>- Complying with applicable regulations
>- Building stronger supplier relationships to encourage favourable contracts and service

## Procurement strategy framework
1. **Develop category profile**
	- Identify the volumes spent in products and services, specification details, current prices and suppliers
	- Identify your customer requirements
2. **Develop sourcing strategy**
	- Select you go-to-market approach
	- Identify which strategy better fits the type of service you're sourcing
3. **Generate supplier portfolio**
	- Research all possible suppliers
	- Develop criteria for supplier selection
4. **Select implementation plan**
	- Develop RFP templates
	- Develop RFP criteria and weightings
5. **Negotiate & select suppliers**
	- Conduct the RFP process and analyse the responses
	- Negotiation process
6. **Implement agreements**
	- Prepare a transition plan for new suppliers
	- Manage implementation of any new suppliers
7. **Continuous improvement**
	- Track the performance metrics
	- Incorporate lessons learned for the next strategic sourcing process

# Differentiation by types
**Direct**
Direct procurement refers to purchasing goods and materials used to produce a company’s final product.

**Indirect**
Indirect procurement covers goods and services that support daily operations but don’t become part of the final product. 

**Goods procurement**
Goods procurement focuses on acquiring physical, tangible items. These goods may be used directly in production or consumed internally across the organization.

**Services procurement**
Services procurement involves acquiring intangible services rather than physical items.

**Raw materials vs. finished goods**
Raw materials are unprocessed inputs, such as metals or chemicals, while finished goods are ready-to-use products such as office furniture or packaged equipment.

# Differentiation by category
Procurement categories are groups of goods or services that share similar traits. They help you organize and streamline your procurement process.

Think of them as a clear procurement categories list. This list helps you group similar purchases. For example, you could create a category for office supplies.

You could also make another category for IT services.

Defining procurement categories provides a structured approach to procurement management, replacing the chaos of unorganized processes.

## Key benefits
**Enhanced Operational Efficiency**: A well-structured procurement system streamlines operations.

**Informed Decision-Making**: Clear categories provide better visibility into spending patterns.

**Improved Procurement Strategies**: Organized categories allow for targeted strategies, optimizing supplier relationships and cost management.

**Smarter Purchase Management**: Proper organization helps identify areas for cost savings and improve overall procurement efficiency.

# Differentiation by product type
The Kraljic Matrix is one of the most effective ways to deliver accurate supplier segmentation. Supply items should be mapped against two key dimensions: risk and profitability.

Risk relates to the likelihood for an unexpected event in the supply chains to disrupt operations.

Profitability describes the impact of a supply item upon the bottom line.

For certain areas of spend, such as stationery, supplies have only a negligible effect on profits. In other categories, a single source of supply can make or break a business.

# Logistical efficiency
Logistics efficiency refers to the ability to optimize the flow of goods, services, and information within a supply chain, aiming to reduce costs, improve speed, and enhance the accuracy of delivery.

Is there a balance between what the customers get = "*the delivery service*" and the "*company's cost of the delivery service*",  the logistical costs.

Logistics efficiency refers to the ability to optimize the flow of goods, services, and information within a supply chain, aiming to reduce costs, improve speed, and enhance the accuracy of delivery.

Logistics efficiency refers to optimizing processes like inventory management, transportation, and order fulfillment to minimize waste and reduce delays.

By streamlining/optimizing these operations, businesses can deliver products faster and more cost-effectively. This efficiency helps companies maintain a competitive edge by ensuring smooth operations and high customer satisfaction.

Logistics efficiency is essential for reducing operational costs and maximizing resource utilization, directly impacting the profitability of a business.

By improving logistics operations, companies can achieve faster delivery times, reduce stockouts or overstocking, and minimize transportation costs. This results in enhanced customer satisfaction and a stronger bottom line.

Assessment of logistical efficiency makes it possible to identify logistical elements that can improve the company's competitiveness on both the service and cost side.

# SCM network supply chains
Companies that contribute to "*from raw material to end customer*" in the same supply chain

Modern supply chains are increasingly customer-driven, prioritizing speed, visibility, and service to meet demand, yet they must remain cost-effective to be sustainable. While cost efficiency is a major focus for profitability, top-performing organizations focus on customer needs to drive loyalty and revenue. A balanced approach is ideal, leveraging technology for agility rather than just low costs.

Cost minimisation or customer-oriented depends on whether service, delivery time or quality is most important?

## Customer driven
**Focus**: Customer service, product availability, personalization, and speed.

**Characteristics**: Uses real-time data to adjust inventory, enabling omnichannel and fast delivery.

**Benefits**: Increased customer satisfaction, higher loyalty, and improved market responsiveness.

**Sustainability**: Customer-centricity is often paired with sustainability, increasing brand value.

## Cost driven
**Focus**: Minimizing costs (*transportation, labour, inventory*) and maximizing internal efficiency.

**Characteristics**: Traditional approach, often focused on bulk shipments and lower-cost production locations.

**When Used**: Primarily prioritized during economic downturns, high inflation, or when optimizing mature products, though it risks reduced service levels.

## Value driven
Modern businesses find success by combining both approaches, often termed "*value-driven*" or "*customer-centric*" to create a sustainable, profitable model.

**Balance**: According to and, successful retailers align with customer demand (*customer-driven*) to increase loyalty, while re-engineering processes to remove unnecessary costs (*cost-driven*).

**Data-Driven**: Utilizing technology to improve demand planning reduces inventory costs while simultaneously improving product availability.

While cost remains crucial, the trend is heavily favoured toward customer-driven models that use technology and data to achieve cost-efficiency without compromising service
