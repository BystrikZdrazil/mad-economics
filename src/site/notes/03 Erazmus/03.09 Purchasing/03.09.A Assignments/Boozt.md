# Supporting information
ESG and CSR are closely related, but not the same and are used in different ways.

CSR (*Corporate Social Responsibility*) is the broader, older term. It’s about a company’s ethical responsibility to society.
- Treating employees fairly
- Supporting communities
- Reducing environmental harm
- Acting ethically beyond just making profit

CSR is often values-driven and voluntary, and it can include initiatives like charity work, sustainability programs, or ethical sourcing. It’s sometimes more about intent and philosophy than strict measurement.

ESG (*Environmental, Social, Governance*) is more recent and more data-driven.

It focuses on measuring and evaluating how a company performs in specific areas:
- **Environmental**: emissions, energy use, climate impact
- **Social**: labour practices, diversity, human rights
- **Governance**: board structure, transparency, anti-corruption

ESG is mainly used by investors and regulators to assess risk and long-term sustainability. It relies on metrics, reporting standards, and comparability.

The connection
- **CSR** = the philosophy and actions (*“we want to be a responsible company”*)
- **ESG** = the measurable framework (*“here’s how we prove and track it”*)

CSR often feeds into ESG—what a company does under CSR can later be quantified and reported as ESG data to the sustainability section in the annual report

# Assignment elaboration
## Influence on Boozt
- Transition from voluntary **CSR** to data-driven **ESG** serves as a core business strategy
- Sustainability is leveraged to optimize operational efficiency and reduce logistics costs through lower return rates
- **ESG** metrics are used to establish brand authority and trust within the nordic consumer market
- High **ESG** scores ensure better access to capital and mitigate risks associated with tightening regulations

## Main Focus Areas of Boozt Today
- Care-for strategy →
    - Roadmap designed to balance commercial growth with corporate responsibility
    - Divided into four primary dimensions and twelve specific objectives

### Environment
- Achieving carbon neutrality across Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions
- Usage of renewable energy at the **Ängelholm** fulfillment centre
- Implementation of the **Fair Use policy** to pause accounts with extreme return behavior and reduce CO2
- Promotion of circularity and reduction of packaging waste to align with the 2026 **EU Circular Economy Act**

### Social
- Focus on employee well-being through the **Care-Why** culture
- Maintenance of diversity and inclusion within the workforce to reflect the customer base

### Governance
- Supply chain transparency maintained through digital due diligence tools for brand partners
- Prioritization of data security as a fundamental ethical responsibility

## Boozt Dilemmas
- Tension between continuous revenue growth and the absolute environmental footprint of increased orders
- Conflict between customer centricity and environmental responsibility regarding the **Fair Use policy**
- Difficulty in maintaining control over the sustainability practices of thousands of third-party brands at scale
- Trade-off between providing enough packaging to prevent item damage and the goal of zero waste
