As an employee in Boozt’s procurement department, looking ahead to **June 1, 2026**, we are in a "playing offense" phase. Based on our 2025 results, we have already achieved an MSCI AAA rating and have 54% of our suppliers committed to Science Based Targets (*SBTi*).

To push Boozt even further, our procurement strategy from June 2026 must transition from "measuring impact" to "enforcing circularity and radical transparency."

# Procurement Plan: "Beyond Compliance" (*Starting June 1, 2026*)
**Goal:** To transform Boozt from a Nordic retail leader into a global benchmark for circular and ethical procurement by 2028.

## Area 1: Supply Chain Transparency & Ethics (*Social/ESG*)
- **The Improvement:** Move from Tier 1 (*brand partners*) to Tier 2 and 3 (*fabric mills and raw materials*) visibility.
- **Operational Plan:**
    - **Phase 1 (*Audit*):** Use our "One Retail Hub" tool to require all brands making up the top 80% of our revenue to map their Tier 2 suppliers by Dec 2026.
    - **Phase 2 (*Enforcement*):** From June 1, any new brand onboarding must provide a "Digital Product Passport" (*DPP*) readiness plan.
    - **Action:** Evaluate and contract a 3rd-party satellite-mapping provider to monitor deforestation risks in leather and cotton sourcing for private label brands.

## Area 2: Radical Circularity in Packaging (*Environment/CSR*)
- **The Improvement:** Moving from "*recycled plastic*" to "*zero-virgin, fiber-only, or reusable*" systems.
- **Operational Plan:**
    - **Phase 1 (*Supplier Phase-out*):** Issue a 6-month notice to packaging suppliers to eliminate all fossil-based virgin plastics.
    - **Phase 2 (*Innovation*):** Identify and pilot a "*Reusable Bag*" system for the Greater Copenhagen and Stockholm areas.
    - **Goal:** By Jan 2027, 40% of Nordic shipments should be in reusable or home-compostable fibre packaging.

## Area 3: Logistics & Last Mile Sourcing (*Carbon/ESG*)
- **The Improvement:** Procurement of delivery services must transition from "*carbon-neutral*" (*offsets*) to "fossil-free" (*actual reduction*).
- **Operational Plan:**
    - **Sourcing:** Renegotiate contracts with PostNord, Bring, and Instabee.
    - **Constraint:** 100% of urban deliveries must be fossil-free by June 2027.
    - **Evaluation:** Implement a "*Sustainability Weight*" in carrier tenders — 40% of the decision score will be based on their EV-fleet percentage and route optimization AI.

## Area 4: Non-Trade Procurement (*Internal Operations*)
- **The Improvement:** Sustainable office and fulfilment center operations.
- **Operational Plan:**
    - **Energy:** Since the Fulfillment Centre is at 98%+ renewable energy, the focus shifts to the new Copenhagen HQ. Negotiate a Power Purchase Agreement (*PPA*) to ensure 100% of the HQ’s energy is from new Nordic wind/solar additions.
    - **IT Sourcing:** Implement a "Circular IT" policy. 80% of new laptop/hardware purchases must be "refurbished-first" or have a guaranteed 5-year lifecycle with a manufacturer buy-back clause.

# Team Identification: Areas of Concern (*Risks*)
1. **Supplier Price Fatigue:** As we demand Tier 2 transparency and SBTi commitments, smaller Nordic brands may struggle with the administrative costs, leading to potential brand churn or price increases for customers.
2. **Logistics Infrastructure Lag:** While we want fossil-free delivery, the charging infrastructure in rural Northern Sweden or Norway may not be ready by 2027, creating a "two-speed" service level.
3. **Data Quality (*Greenwashing*):** Reliance on self-reported data through "One Retail Hub" remains a risk. We need a robust "verification" budget (procuring independent audits) to ensure our "Made with Care" label remains trustworthy.
4. **Inventory Liquidation vs. Sustainability:** Our "Booztlet" (*outlet*) model is great for circularity (*no burning clothes*), but we must ensure we aren't procuring "overstock" just to feed the outlet, which would incentivize overproduction.

# Suggestions for "Tomorrow"
- **AI for Procurement:** Use our AI infrastructure to predict "sustainable demand." If AI shows a shift toward linen over polyester, we should proactively shift our procurement spend 12 months in advance.
- **Vendor Diversity:** Create a "Nordic Emerging Talent" procurement fund to support small, local, sustainable designers who can't yet meet our high-volume requirements.
