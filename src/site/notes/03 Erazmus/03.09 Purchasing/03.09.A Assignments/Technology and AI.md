# Technology
**S2P (*source-to-pay*) software** will be one of the most important things in procurement, as it allows to have **everything in one place** from looking at different potential partners to creating an invoice.

**IoT and big data** will also surely play a big role in the future. This allows for e.g. **smart systems in warehouses**, tracking how many units there are with changes made in real time. If done correctly, this system would **almost 100% eliminate** the **human error** factor from the equation.

[Google beam](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/research/project-starline-google-beam-update/) is also a really cool project that helps make **online meetings more user-friendly** but primarily **more personal**. This would help eliminate the awkwardness of online meetings today while still **bringing in the personal factor**, where a person feels as if the other party is sitting directly in front of them across the table.

# AI
Regarding procurement in general, AI could really help with **managing data**, **analysing trends** that may take a human too long to spot, **predicting the market conditions** based on 24/7 monitoring and **comparing** it to **historical data** and so much more.

Even today it is so **quick in looking up information** and **finding patterns** that if it continues in this rate, people will be **relying on it for a lot of business tasks,** which is a double-edged sword however.

Another way AI can help with procurement is just by **analysing** a lot of **data** and then **explaining the result** to a **human** that can add their own mind to the mix, combining the **best of both worlds**.
