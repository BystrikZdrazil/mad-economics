# The issue
During the COVID period, there were a lot of problems regarding public procurement in Slovakia. Many of which could have been avoided, but some of which were inevitable, closely tied to how the Slovak system was set up.

The main problem of public procurement during this period was the lack of medicine during some weeks. There may have been months when we had most of what we needed, but sometimes we couldn't even get antibiotics, as there was simply no supply anywhere.

# The challenge
The main challenge here was how the state failed to secure contracts with big pharmacies to have a constant supply of life-saving medicine.

This could have been avoided by our politicians, if they tried more to ensure the safety of their citizens instead of persuading everyone that COVID is a hoax and the only thing that can save us is the Russian vaccine.

# System failure
Slovakia faces significant healthcare challenges due to underfunding in preventive care. Despite per capita spending rising from **€12 in 2019 to €56 in 2023/2024**, this remains just **50% of the EU average** — far behind top spenders like Germany (*€260*) and Austria (*€208*) (*Globsec, 2024*).

Consequently, Slovakia reports poor health outcomes, including:
- **High mortality rates** from preventable (*262 per 100,000*) and treatable (*169 per 100,000*) causes—nearly double the EU average.
- **Lower life expectancy** at birth (*77.2 years in 2022*) compared to the EU average (*80.7 years*).

However, the specific impact of Emergency Care Systems (*ECS*) on these outcomes remains poorly understood. To build a stronger healthcare system, more data is needed regarding patient trajectories, barriers and facilitators to efficient ECS, and the perspectives of both healthcare professionals (*HCPs*) and patients.
