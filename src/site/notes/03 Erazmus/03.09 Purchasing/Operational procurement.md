# Operational
- **Standard items** that do not require framework agreements
	- Develop guidelines for purchasing products 
- **Non-standard items**
	- Create contracts and framework agreements

# Other
- Who are the best suppliers? 
- Which factors are most important when selecting a supplier? 
- How is the competition among suppliers? 
- New needs in the company? 
- Changes in the company? 
- Re-negotiation of contracts?

**Purchasing approach depends on item types**
- Consumables, office supplies
	- Inventory is replenished
- Assets, machinery, machine party, vehicles
	- Need arises due to strategic/tactical decisions
- Raw materials, components, semi-finished goods, energy
	- Ongoing attention to renegotiation/supplier switching because they represent major costs in the company

# Delivery
- Time
- Quantity
- Location

# Purchase type
- **New purchase**: not previously purchased
- **Repeat purchase**: previously purchased
- Modified repeat purchase
- **Routine purchase**: agreements already exist

Purchase types affect the purchasing process

# The sourcing process
1. Identification of the requirement
2. Crafting the sourcing plan
3. Conduction of market analysis
4. Pre-qualification of suppliers
5. Evaluating supply offers
6. Creating the contract or supplier relationship

Using a real-world supermarket case study (*barcode scanners for warehouse operations*), you'll see exactly how procurement professionals apply these stages in practice.
