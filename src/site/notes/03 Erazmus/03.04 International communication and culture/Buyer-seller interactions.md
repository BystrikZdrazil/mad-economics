- These are **structured exchanges**, ranging from **transactional** to **long-term** partnerships
- **Communication style** and **content compatibility** determine success
- These relationships involve **trust**, **power dynamics**, and **information sharing**
- Digital platforms are **reducing traditional gaps** and changing how trust is established

# Key dynamics of interaction
**Initial perception**: Judgements are formed in as little as one-tenth of a second to 30 seconds.

**Buyer perspective**: Often sceptical, viewing sellers as overly optimistic, they seek value and high-quality solutions to pain points.

**Seller perspective**: Perceives buyers as reserved and calculating, often leading to more strategic or aggressive sales tactics.

**Relationship quality**: High-quality relationships enhance trust, while low-quality interactions lead to transactional, short-term exchanges

# Selling orientations
![ICC_selling_orientations.png](/img/user/06%20Images/ICC_selling_orientations.png)

- **Hard sell** is an **aggressive**, **direct**, and **short-term** approach focused on immediate sales, often using high-pressure tactics and urgency
- **Soft sell** is an **indirect**, **polite**, and **long-term** strategy emphasizing relationship-building, trust, and honesty

# Differentiating sales persuasion
- *How far can one take persuasion without becoming insistent and irritating?*
- *Is one persuasive merely by listening?*
- *What arguments will be the best and the quickest for persuading the prospective purchaser?*

# Summary of influence on culture

| **Cultural value**        | **Influence on seller and buyer**                                                                                                             |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| *Inferior status for trade* | Poor status for sales; selling is reserved to a minority group                                                                                |
| *Being/doing*               | More personal relationship orientated; more impersonal deal orientated                                                                        |
| *Money*                     | Price bargaining as friendship ritual                                                                                                         |
| *Ingroup orientation*       | Only people from a certain ingroup are considered as adequate for sales roles; transactions have to be made preferably across ingroup members |
| *Family orientation*        | Buyer–seller relationships are viewed as an element of a larger family network                                                                |
| *Short-term orientation*    | Achieving the sale is the paramount goal                                                                                                      |
| *Long-term orientation*     | Keeping the client is the paramount goal                                                                                                      |
| *Low/high power distance*   | Equality/inequality between buyer and seller                                                                                                  |
| *Listening versus talking*  | Soft sell versus hard sell                                                                                                                    |
