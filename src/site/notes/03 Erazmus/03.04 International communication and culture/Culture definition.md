# Culture and marketing

## Functionalist/Etic approach
- Culture **one of many factors** to be aware of
- It is difficult to relate to culture, usually a checklist of **cultural elements** is used
	- Religion
	- Language
	- Family
- The idea that culture can be **divided into elements**, which you can relate to in an objective way (*Like ”Hofsteede”*)
- Culture can be measured (*Objective*)

## Interpretive/Emic approach
- Culture is a **superior concept**, which provides relations and meanings
- Perspective becomes intercultural
- The **description** of culture will **depend on the background** of the described
- This makes the description more **dynamic**
- Culture **has to be experienced** (*subjective*)

# Culture definitions
- Culture is the **link between individuals** and **societies**:
	- **Learned behaviour** and results of behaviour shared and transmitted by the **members** of a particular **society**.
- Culture as a way to **define human behaviour**:
	- A whole **way of life**, **material**, **intellectual** and **spiritual**.
- Useful to, but not a characteristic of, **individuals**:
	- Culture is a **set of beliefs or standards** (*control mechanisms*), **shared** by a **group of people**, which help the **individual** decide what is, what can be, how to feel, what to do and how to go about doing it.

- Provides a **set of beliefs** and **standards**
	- **What to do** and **how to do** it **correctly**
		- What is **palatable**, **admissible**, **ethical**, magical, religious, hygienic, quality, etc.
		- When it is **time to sleep**, to **eat**, to **work**, etc.
- **Mindsets** or **mental maps** guide behavior
- Prejudices and **stereotypes**: what, why and how
- Ethnocentric behaviors

## Functions of culture
- Communication codes
- **Long-term survival**
- Synchronization of behaviors
	- explicit – implicit
- "*Best way*" for **doing** or **not doing** things (*pools of knowledge*)
- Identity (*ingroups versus outgroups*)
- Vision of the world (*Weltanschauung*)
	- Strongly related to deep-seated assumptions in particular languages.

# Cultural perspectives
Culture can be approached from three basic overall perspectives
1. **Consumer culture** 
	- Important when you sell to foreign customers 
	- Will affect the marketing program, including communication
2. **Business culture** 
	- Important when you trade with foreign companies 
	- Will affect negotiation, business relations etc.
3. **Working culture** 
	- Important when you have foreign employees

# 4 Major elements of culture
- Language and communication
- Institutions
- Material productions
- Symbolic productions

## Language and communication
- Primary mechanism to convey and share information
- Includes both explicit and implicit elements
- Approximately 6000 languages
	- 389 are spoken by more than 1 million people

## Institutions
Based on:
- **Reproduction** (*e.g. blood and marriage*)
- **Territoriality** (*e.g. neighbourhoods*)
- **Physiological** (*e.g. sex, age*)
- **Spontaneous** *(e.g. common interests/goals*)
- **Occupational** (*e.g. expertise*)
- **Hierarchy** (*e.g. status and rank*)
- **Totality** *(e.g. political processes*)

## Material productions
- Transmit, reproduce, update and improve knowledge and skills
- Primary material productions include:
	- **Artistic** (*e.g. music, art*)
	- **Intellectual** (*e.g. books*)
	- **Physical** (*e.g. buildings, tools, products*)
	- **Service** (*e.g. education, media, banks*)

## Symbolic productions
- Religious/moral beliefs
- Life after death?
- Symbol conveys more than the object
	- (*e.g., road signs, wedding rings, skull and crossbones*)
- Symbolic meaning in many things
	- (*e.g., colour, shape, labels, brands, numbers*)
- Quick understanding

# Approaching culture
**Ethnocentrism**
- Belief in superiority of own culture; tendency to see own culture as the centre, and measure others by it

**Self-reference criterion**
- Judge others according to our own normative standards

**Racism**
- Believe some humans are inferior (*intelligence, creative ability, moral sense...*) because of their race
 
**Cultural hostility**
- Does not necessarily imply racism
	- Cultural differences
	- Territorial conflicts
	- Economic competition
