**Local** is when companies change their products based on the market.

**Global** is when companies have the the same products for all markets.

# Standardization (*global*)
- An **ethnocentric** approach (*what works in the home country will work abroad*)
- A given message is **perceived alike** by **all** receivers
- Related to the **transmission paradigm** (*messages are clear and does not require context or interpretation*)

# Adaptation (*local*)
- Big cultural difference calls for **adjustment of product presentation** and rhetorical strategies (*language, style, persuasive strategies, photos*)
- Use of **national** or **local characteristics**
- **Avoid unclear text** or **content** which might be **provocative**

# Standardization or adaptation

| **Pros of standardization**            | **Pros of adaptation**                             |
| ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Cost savings                       | Different values in different cultures         |
| Enhanced quality                   | Differences in consumer behaviour              |
| Coordination and control is easier | Different experiences                          |
| A global image can be created      | Different education levels                     |
| Global segments do exist           | Products in different stages of the life cycle |

# Advertising solves problems
**Objectives**
- Brand awareness
- Brand image
- Provoke trial/buy
- Increase sales
- Build loyalty

**Consumer decision-making**
- **Need recognition**
- **Information search**
	- Moderated by knowledge, involvement, individual differences and uncertainty
- **Information evaluation**
	- Attribute evaluation, simple heuristics and affective process or feelings
- **Purchase decision**
	- Situational variables, point of purchase sales or advertising

# Attitudes towards advertising
General attitudes towards the role and functions of advertising

**What does advertising do**
- Promote unnecessary needs?
- Is it hidden persuasion?
- Does it increase primary demand for addictive products such as cigarettes?
- Is advertising socially useful to inform people and increase competition?
- Does it increase or decrease prices?

**Attitudes depend on social responses to**
- What is the social function of advertising?
- What are the prevailing arguments concerning the legitimacy of comparative advertising?
- How should competition between brands be facilitated?
- Does comparative advertising result in fooling the consumer by using disputable information to praise one's own brand and put down others?

# Advertising as communication
Advertising stratefy comprises "*what is said*" and execution concerns more "*how it is said*" (*who "says" the message in which setting?*)
- Relates to the core element of the message
- The types of appeals used
- The themes developed
- The overall communication style, whether
	- Direct or indirect
	- Explicit or implicit ([[03 Erazmus/03.04 International communication and culture/Halls communication model\|Halls communication model]])
	- [[03 Erazmus/03.04 International communication and culture/Trompenaar’s cultural dimensions#3. Neutral vs. Affective\|Neutral or affective]]

# Successful global advertising
- Brand's core position must be relevant in each country
- Expression of core position must work in each market
- What is said is the same but how you say it can be different
- Core themes can be globalized
