>[!example]+
>**Language barriers** can make communication difficult and lead to misunderstandings
>
>**Different customs and traditions** can cause conflict or confusion
>
>**Different values and beliefs** can create tension or disagreement
>
>**Different ways of doing things** can lead to frustration or inefficiency
>
>**Different styles of dress** or **mannerisms** can be distracting or off-putting

# Conflict de-escalation
- Debrief the communication misunderstandings
- Talk rather frankly (*if possible*) about the personality clash so that it is detrimental to the task at hand
- Focus on a joint doing orientation favouring task completion
- Reach an agreement upon the ways to proceed (*mindset issues*)

# Intercultural courtesy
**Research norms**: before interacting, learn the basics of greetings, dining, and hierarchy in the other person's culure

**Observe and Mirror**: when in doubt, observe your counterparts and mirror their approach, particularly in greetings or dining situations

**Practise active listening**: pay attention to both verbal and non-verbal cues (such as silence or hesitant "maybes") that may indicate discomfort or disagreement

**Use Clear and Simple Language**: avoid idiomatic expressions, slang, or complex metaphors to ensure understanding

**Be Patient with Time Perception**: understand that punctuality is viewed differently across cultures, with some placing strict importance on it (e.g., Switzerland, Japan) while others are more flexible

# Different types of rationalities
Advice based on a **positivist approach** might be looking at **people and relationships** as they are, search for empirical cues, facts and evidence (*individualistic countries - rational*)

Advice based on a **normative approach** might be **understanding your partner**, put yourself in their shoes nad separate interests from people (*Collectivist countries - emotional*)
