# Cross-cultural IM key aspects
**Local influencer collaboration**: Partnering with local creators is crucial for authentic brand promotion that resonates with local interests.

**Cultural sensitivity and adaptation**: Marketers must adapt messages to local values, traditional, and behaviours to ensure relevance. This includes understanding high-context versus low-context communication.

**Individualism VS Collectivism**: While Western markets often emphasize individualistic messages, Asian markets may respond better to collectivist approaches.

**Content Customization**: Visual elements, such as colours and gestures, should be adapted to fit local aesthetics and avoid unintended offensive meanings.

# Global campaigns considerations
**Research local markets**: Understand audience preferences and expectations before launching campaigns.

**Clear guidelines**: Provide clear guidelines to influencers to maintain brand consistency while allowing for local nuance.

**Platform variation**: Influencer impact varies by platform and region, with TikTok, for instance, showing different engagement drivers across culture.

**Managing trust**: Influencer credibility is key to breaking through in new markets. Influencer attractiveness can positively boost trust, whereas expertise might have a different impact
