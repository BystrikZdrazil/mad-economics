# American boss -> Japanese subordinates
- Not that great of a difference in power distance
- Vastly different individualism
- Pretty different masculinity score

**Bottom line**: Thee boss needs to focus on promoting less individualistic goals in the company (*No competitions between employees*) and also needs to adjust their views on masculinity.

# Japanese boss -> French subordinates
- Quite similar power distance
- Greatly different individualism
- Vastly different masculinity score

**Bottom line**: Thee boss needs to adjust their views on individualism, providing a more individual work environment, not viewing the workplace as a whole, and additionally needs to provide space for less masculine values to still work.

# French boss -> Swedish subordinates
- Pretty different power distance
- Same individualism score
- Very different masculinity score

**Bottom line**: Thee boss needs to adjust their view on power distance, making it more less formal and become more approachable. They also need to adjust the masculinity views, as the Swedes are very feminine in this regard.

# Swedish boss -> Japanese subordinates
- Quite similar power distance
- Pretty different individualism score
- Hugely different masculinity score

**Bottom line**: Thee boss can keep their views and practices regarding power distance, however, they need to alter the perception of individualism, as the Japanese like more of a societal progress instead of the benefit of one. Lastly, there is a huge difference between the masculinity scores, where Sweden is fairly feminine and Japan is almost totally masculine.
