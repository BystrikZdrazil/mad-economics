# Danish and Chinese BATNA
>[!todo]+ Task
>**Scenario**: A Danish tech company (*low-context, short term focus*) in negotiationg a joint venture with a family-owned Chinese company (*high-context, long-term relationship focus*)
>- **Danish BATNA**: a contract with a competitor in Singapore
>- **Chinese BATNA**: a new agreement with a local partner
>
>**Questions**:
>1. How should the Danish team present their alternative without damaging the long-term relationship?
>2. How might the Chinese team's focus on trust affect the strength of the Danish BATNA?
>3. Discuss the three frameworks. What framework would you recommend for the Danish team?
>
>---
>
>[[03 Erazmus/03.04 International communication and culture/03.04.A Assignments/DK and CH BATNA\|DK and CH BATNA]]

</div></div>


1. The alternative may be presented as something necessary, if the deal doesn't go their way. The Chinese will then view it as something the company doesn't want to do, but is forced to in order to survive
2. The focus on trust may affect the deal negatively and also positively
	- Negatively when the Danish company didn't tell the Chinese company beforehand and the Chinese may take it as a betrayal of loyalty
	- Positively when the Danish company did tell the Chinese company in time, so the Chinese may value the openness and honesty, which in return makes them value the company maybe even more
3. I would choose personally the Framework 1 (*Greek style*), as it is the only one I understand yet, as of writing this answer
