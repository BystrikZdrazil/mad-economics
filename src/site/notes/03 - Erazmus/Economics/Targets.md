What is measured usually is also achieved easier. Targets establish the level of performance or rate of improvement required for a measure (KPI)
- Typically **set by management**
- Should be set to **represent excellent performance**
- Should, if achieved, place the company as one of the **best performers in its industry**
- Should create **distinctive value for customers**

Targets are important, as without a clear goal it can not be achieved.

# Targets and timeframes
- **Long term targets**
	- Monumental challenges
	- Takes years to conquer (10-30)
	- Cannot be achieved through business as usual
- **Midrange targets**
	- Breaking the long term targets into smaller segments
	- 3-5 years
	- Realistic but still motivating
- **Short term targets**
	- *“A journey of 1000 miles begins with a single step”*
	- Annual basis (or even shorter)
	- Early warning system, timely feedback

# Sources of target information
- **Employees**
	- Unique knowledge on internal processes
- **Trend and baselines**
	- With historical data and statistical techniques
- **Interview with executives**
- **SWOT analysis**
	- Required targets to maximise opportunities and minimise threats
- **Industry averages**
	- Industry or trade organization that measure performance across industries
- **Benchmarking**
	- Examine best in class

# Putting the [[03 - Erazmus/Economics/Balanced scorecard\|BSC]] into action
- Having strategic goals and having identified KPI’s (and targets) is not enough
- In order to change something you need to act
- We need to develop and prioritize strategic initiatives that can help us reach our targets

- Match them with strategic objectives
- Develop what is missing and consider eliminating non strategic initiatives
- Prioritize remaining initiatives (rank them to make resource allocation decisions)

# Cascading a balance scorecard
It means developing a [[03 - Erazmus/Economics/Balanced scorecard\|balanced scorecard]] at each level of an organisation

The [[03 - Erazmus/Economics/Balanced scorecard\|BSC]] of Business Unites, divisions etc. should be aligned with the highest level scorecard

The idea is to identify strategic objectives and measures that lowe-level departments and groups will use to track their progress and contribution to overall goals. So every scorecard must link back to overall objectives.

"Lowest" level = personal balanced scorecards

# Using [[03 - Erazmus/Economics/Balanced scorecard\|BSC]] to link strategy and budget
The [[03 - Erazmus/Economics/Balanced scorecard\|Balanced Scorecard]] presents an opportunity to link budgets with strategy

With a cascaded [[03 - Erazmus/Economics/Balanced scorecard\|BSC]] as guide managers can solicit feedback and involvement of employees

The cascaded Scorecards are the fundaments for planning strategic investments; besides from including objectives, measures and targets, they should also include strategic initiatives that often require investments. They form the basis of budget submissions.

The advantage: spending is dictated by the ability to influence strategic goals rather than a simple recalculation of the previous year’s submissions.

# Barriers of effective use
Senior management is not commited

Scorecard responsibilities do not filter down

The solution is over designed, or the scorecard is a one-time event

The scorecard is treated as a systems or a consulting project
